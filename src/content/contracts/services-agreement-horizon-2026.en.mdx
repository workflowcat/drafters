# Master Services Agreement

> **Briefing before negotiation.** Horizon is engineering-led and IP-obsessive:
> their Head of Engineering reads this line-by-line and leaves margin notes.
> Expect pressure on three sections. **§4 Intellectual Property** — they will
> ask for source escrow and full assignment on delivery, not on payment.
> **§7 Liability** — they will push the cap up to 2× fees, not down.
> **§3 Payment** — their standard is 14 days, which we accept; watch for
> any attempt to net 14 against "acceptance," which converts the clock
> into a gate. Everything else usually closes clean in one round.

<Voice voice="business">
14-day terms are Horizon's standard and fine for our cash flow. The
line to defend is against slippage: any amendment that adds "subject
to acceptance" or "upon receipt of a compliant invoice" turns 14 days
into 30 in practice. Push the approval gate into the SoW, not into
the payment clock.
</Voice>

<Voice voice="negotiations">
Rate-review cap at 10% is the ceiling, not the default. Our typical
annual move is 5–7%. If Horizon pushes for 0% (fixed for the term),
counter with "0% in year 1, renegotiate at 12 months" — gives them
budget certainty without locking us out of a correction when inflation
moves.
</Voice>

<Voice voice="legal">
"Monthly in arrears" is deliberate. Moving to milestone billing turns
this MSA into a fixed-price shape, which interacts badly with the IP
clause (§4) — the "as payments are made" assignment language assumes
a steady payment cadence, not a stage-gated one. If Horizon wants
milestones on a specific SoW, handle it inside that SoW, not here.
</Voice>

## 2. Engagement Structure

Each engagement under this Agreement shall be described in a
**Statement of Work** (hereinafter, "**SoW**") signed by both
Parties. The SoW shall describe, at minimum, the scope of Services,
the assigned team or rate card, the fees and billing model, any
applicable milestones or acceptance criteria, and the planned
duration.

In case of conflict between this Agreement and a SoW, **this
Agreement prevails**, except where the SoW expressly states that a
specific provision of this Agreement is varied for that SoW only.

A SoW may be terminated independently of this Agreement on the notice
period stated in the SoW. Termination of a SoW does not terminate
this Agreement; termination of this Agreement terminates all SoWs
that have not been completed, subject to §8.

<Voice voice="delivery">
The "SoW prevails only if it says so expressly" rule is load-bearing.
Without it, every hurried SoW we sign on a Friday afternoon risks
silently overriding the MSA. In practice: if a partner wants to vary
a §4–§7 term in a SoW, Delivery flags it to Legal before signature —
no exceptions.
</Voice>

## 3. Fees and Payment

<Clause baseId="late-payments-interest" lang="en" />

<Voice voice="interaction">
Two clauses, two mechanisms. Payment dispute is a **process** —
Customer flags the disputed line, pays the undisputed remainder,
and both sides resolve the delta in 5 days. Late interest is a
**consequence** — 0.5%/day after the due date. Together they form
a two-tier system: fair process first, financial pressure second.
Never collapse them into one.
</Voice>

<Voice voice="business">
Late interest rarely gets billed. The value isn't in collection,
it's in the clock starting to run — most finance teams will
prioritise a paying invoice as soon as the interest counter appears
on a statement. Horizon pays on time; this clause exists for when
Horizon is acquired and the buyer's AP takes over.
</Voice>

## 4. Intellectual Property

<Clause baseId="client-ip-assignment" lang="en" />

<Voice voice="legal">
"As payments are made" is the pivot. It means unpaid Deliverables
are not yet assigned — which is our leverage if the Customer stops
paying. Horizon will ask to change this to "on delivery" to get
assignment independent of payment; refuse. If we fully assigned
pre-payment and they then disputed the invoice, we would have no
recovery short of litigation.
</Voice>

<Voice voice="negotiations">
The copyleft carve-out (no GPL/AGPL without consent) is where
engineering-led clients usually engage. Horizon's Head of Engineering
will want the list extended to cover specific licences (SSPL, BUSL,
Elastic License). Accept — the more specific the list, the less
ambiguity at audit. Resist any demand for a per-library attestation
process; that belongs in the SoW, not the MSA.
</Voice>

## 5. Confidentiality

<Clause baseId="confidentiality-mutual" lang="en" />

<Voice voice="legal">
Mutual confidentiality is correct for this engagement. A one-way
NDA (Customer protects its info, Contractor does not protect its)
is common in enterprise templates — decline it. We routinely share
team composition, rate information, and internal process, and that
information should travel in both directions under the same terms.
</Voice>

## 6. Warranties and Disclaimers

The Contractor warrants that (a) it has full authority to enter
into this Agreement and to perform the Services; (b) the Services
will be performed in a professional manner and by personnel with
appropriate skill; (c) to its knowledge the Deliverables do not
infringe any third-party intellectual property rights, subject to
the carve-outs in §4.

Except as expressly stated in this §6, **the Services and
Deliverables are provided "as is"** and the Contractor makes no
other warranties, express or implied, including (without
limitation) any implied warranty of merchantability or fitness
for a particular purpose.

<Voice voice="delivery">
"Professional manner and appropriate skill" is the operational
warranty. In practice this is the clause that gets invoked when
a junior engineer ships a production incident — the Customer
points here, we point to the incident post-mortem and the senior
review trail. Keep the incident log; it is the evidence that
this warranty was honoured.
</Voice>

## 7. Limitation of Liability

<Clause baseId="liability-limitation-mutual" lang="en" />

<Voice voice="negotiations">
The default cap equals the fees paid or payable in the 12 months
preceding the claim. Horizon engineering will push for **2× fees**
or a fixed floor (e.g. €250k). Our order of concessions: first
offer to extend the lookback from 12 to 24 months (keeps the cap
proportional to engagement size); next, accept 1.5× fees; last,
accept a fixed floor only if paired with a matching ceiling. Never
uncap liability for indemnities outside the IP and confidentiality
carve-outs.
</Voice>

<Voice voice="business">
Every €1 of liability cap above 12 months' fees is a €1 we are
carrying on our insurance. When Horizon pushes the cap, the answer
is not "no" — the answer is "let us bring our broker in." That
moves the conversation from negotiation to underwriting, where the
numbers are real.
</Voice>

## 8. Term and Termination

<Clause baseId="termination-at-will" lang="en" />

<Voice voice="interaction">
Termination at 30 days' notice interacts with §4 IP assignment:
work-in-progress that has not been invoiced at the time of notice
is not assigned. During the notice period, either the Customer
pays for the in-flight work and takes assignment, or the Contractor
delivers only what was already paid for. Flag this to the Delivery
lead before any notice is given — missing the handoff costs more
than the 30 days.
</Voice>

## 9. Governing Law and Disputes

<Clause baseId="dispute-resolution-uk" lang="en" />

<Voice voice="legal">
England and Wales is a deliberate neutral choice between Germany
(Contractor's seat) and Finland (Customer's seat). Neither side is
home, both sides' counsel can work with the forum, and the 60-day
negotiation window before court gives both sides a real window to
settle. If Horizon proposes Finland, counter with England. If they
propose arbitration, check with insurance first — some policies
treat arbitration awards differently than court judgments.
</Voice>

## 10. General

**Notices.** Any notice under this Agreement shall be in writing
and sent to the registered addresses stated above, with a copy by
email to the designated contact for each Party. Notices are
effective on the second Business Day after dispatch.

**Assignment.** Neither Party may assign this Agreement without the
prior written consent of the other, except that either Party may
assign to a successor in a merger, consolidation, or sale of
substantially all assets, on written notice.

**Entire agreement.** This Agreement, together with any SoWs signed
under it, constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties in
respect of its subject matter and supersedes all prior discussions,
proposals, and agreements.

**Counterparts and electronic signature.** This Agreement may be
executed in counterparts, including by electronic signature, each
of which shall be deemed an original and which together shall
constitute one instrument.

**Severability.** If any provision of this Agreement is held
unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full
force, and the Parties shall negotiate a replacement provision
that achieves the intended commercial effect.
