# Statement of Work 01 — Route-Optimization Engineering Support

> **Briefing before negotiation.** This is a T&M engagement under the
> Horizon MSA. The MSA carries the heavy terms (IP, liability,
> confidentiality, payment); this SoW only defines **what we do, who
> does it, at what rate, and for how long**. Two things to watch in
> the first review: Horizon may ask for a **monthly hours cap
> (not just an estimate)** — resist, because a hard cap converts T&M
> into fixed-capacity and makes overruns painful to explain mid-sprint.
> They may also ask for **acceptance gating on sprint deliverables** —
> decline for T&M; acceptance gates belong to fixed-price work, not
> a dedicated-team engagement.

**SoW reference:** SoW-01

**Under:** Master Services Agreement between Acme Services GmbH and
Horizon Maritime Analytics Oy, dated \_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_ (the
"**MSA**"). Defined terms used without definition in this SoW have
the meanings given in the MSA.

**Effective date:** \_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_

**Planned end date:** 12 months from the Effective date, renewing
on the terms set out in §7.

## 1. Summary

| Item | Value |
| :---- | :---- |
| **Engagement** | Route-optimization engineering support for Horizon's vessel-routing product |
| **Billing model** | **Time and Materials (T&M)**, monthly in arrears |
| **Estimated monthly commitment** | ~160 engineering hours, distributed across the team below (indicative, not a cap) |
| **Monthly estimate (EUR)** | **~€33,000**, excluding expenses; actuals billed monthly per §4 |
| **Currency** | EUR |
| **Overtime** | Per MSA §1 (2× Rate, written approval only) |
| **Duration** | 12 months rolling; either Party may terminate this SoW on **14 days' notice** per §7 |

<Voice voice="business">
The €33,000/month figure is an **estimate**, not a commitment. Under
T&M we bill actuals. Horizon's procurement will want to see a PO for
the annualised figure (~€400k) to reserve budget; write that PO
against "up to" language, not "fixed." A fixed PO against a T&M SoW
is a category error that causes disputes six months in.
</Voice>

<Voice voice="negotiations">
Horizon asks for the SoW termination notice to match the MSA's 30 days.
Defend 14 days here: T&M engagements depend on ramp-down flexibility.
If they insist, counter with "14 days for the Customer, 30 days for
the Contractor" — asymmetric notice is reasonable when one side
(us) carries staffing inertia that the other does not.
</Voice>

## 2. Scope of Services

The Contractor shall provide engineering support for the Customer's
vessel-route optimisation product, comprising:

- **Backend engineering** on the route-solver service: performance
  work, algorithmic improvements, integration with new telemetry
  inputs.
- **Data-pipeline work** on the emissions-telemetry ingest: schema
  evolution, back-fills, monitoring.
- **On-call support rotation** for the services the team maintains,
  within the hours stated on the rate card (§3).
- **Pairing and code review** with the Customer's engineering team
  during the engagement.

**Explicitly out of scope** (handled under a separate SoW if needed):

- Front-end engineering on the Customer's dashboard product.
- Mobile-app engineering.
- SRE / platform-level incident response outside the team's services.
- New-product work outside route-optimisation and emissions
  telemetry.

<Voice voice="delivery">
Explicit exclusions are worth their weight in billed hours. Without
them, every Friday-afternoon request ("could someone take a quick
look at the dashboard bug?") turns into scope drift. With them, we
have a crisp answer: "yes, and it's a new SoW." The exclusions list
is a contract-shaped "no" that the Customer's engineering leads can
use internally to protect our focus.
</Voice>

<Voice voice="legal">
Naming the team in Exhibit A is deliberate. Horizon will want a

not add names to this SoW without HR signing off on the risk.
</Voice>

## 4. Billing

**Rate × hours.** Each month, the Contractor invoices for hours
worked at the Rate for the role performing the work, in 0.25-hour
increments.

**Timesheet evidence.** Invoices are accompanied by timesheet
exports showing engineer, role, date, hours, and a one-line task
description, exported from the Contractor's time-tracking system.

**Expenses.** Reasonable travel and out-of-pocket expenses approved
in writing by the Customer in advance are billed at cost, with
receipts.

**Payment mechanics.** Invoicing cadence, payment period, disputes,
and late interest follow the MSA — specifically **MSA §3**. This
SoW does not vary those terms.

<Voice voice="interaction">
This section deliberately does not restate MSA §3. Restating it
creates drift: if MSA §3 is amended at renewal and this SoW still
says "14 days" when the MSA now says "21," the SoW silently
overrides (per MSA §2). Keep SoW billing terse and reference the
MSA; let the MSA be the one place payment rules live.
</Voice>

## 5. Change Requests

**Lightweight change process.** Because this is a T&M engagement,
routine scope adjustments (changing priorities within the stated
scope of §2) do not require a formal change order. The team and
the Customer's engineering lead manage sprint-level priorities
through the normal product-management channels.

**When a Change Request is required.** A written, signed Change
Request is required where the change:

1. Adds work outside the scope defined in §2, or
2. Adds a named role not on the rate card in §3, or
3. Changes the monthly estimate in §1 by more than **±20%**, or
4. Extends the planned end date.

A Change Request shall describe the change, its impact on the
rate card and monthly estimate, and be signed by an authorised
signatory of each Party. Until signed, the Contractor is not
obliged to perform the changed work.

<Voice voice="negotiations">
A fixed-price engagement would compose the
[[Change Order]] clause here — that clause
freezes scope and gates all changes through a written order. For
T&M, that level of ceremony is over-engineered and slows the team
down. The lighter process above is calibrated to the engagement
type. If a future SoW is fixed-price, use the full Change Order
clause instead; they are not interchangeable.
</Voice>

## 6. Acceptance

**T&M — no acceptance gate.** This engagement is not structured
around discrete deliverables against which formal acceptance is
meaningful. Quality is assured through the Warranties in **MSA §6**,
through ongoing pairing and review with the Customer's engineering
team, and through the Customer's right to request rework at no
additional charge where work does not meet the professional
standard stated in MSA §6.

**Continuous feedback.** The Parties shall review progress and
priorities at a standing bi-weekly cadence. Material concerns
raised in that review shall be addressed in the following sprint
at no additional charge, subject to the scope limits of §2.

<Voice voice="delivery">
The absence of an acceptance gate is not a gap — it is the shape
of the engagement. Teams that try to attach
[[Acceptance Criteria]] to a T&M SoW
end up creating a gate that either never fires (because every
sprint has some in-flight work) or fires weekly and creates
administrative overhead that the engagement model cannot absorb.
Use the bi-weekly review; keep the contract quiet.
</Voice>

## 7. Duration and Termination

**Initial term.** 12 months from the Effective date.

**Renewal.** On expiry of the initial term, this SoW renews for
successive 3-month periods unless either Party gives notice of
non-renewal at least 30 days before the end of the then-current
period.

**Termination for convenience.** Either Party may terminate this
SoW on **14 days' written notice**, without cause. This notice
period varies the MSA's 30 days for this SoW only, as permitted by
MSA §2.

**Effect of termination.** On termination, the Contractor shall
complete work-in-progress to a safe handover state, invoice for
hours worked through the termination date, and hand over all
Deliverables in accordance with MSA §4 (Intellectual Property).

<Voice voice="interaction">
The 14-day notice here is the single variation from the MSA, and
it has to be stated expressly per MSA §2. Without the phrase "as
permitted by MSA §2," the 30-day MSA notice would prevail and the
14-day language would be unenforceable. This is why MSA §2 matters:
every SoW deviation has to be loud on the page.
</Voice>

## 8. Variations from the MSA

Except for the termination notice period set out in §7, **no
provision of the MSA is varied by this SoW**. All other terms of
the MSA apply to this engagement in full.
