**Team Composition.** The Contractor shall provide a dedicated team
of the size and skill profile specified in the applicable Team Plan
annexed to this Agreement. The team works exclusively on the
Customer's project during the agreed working hours.

**Monthly Retainer.** The Customer shall pay a fixed monthly
retainer for the agreed headcount, invoiced on the first business
day of each calendar month and due within **fifteen (15) calendar
days**. The retainer covers standard working hours as defined in
the Team Plan.

**Ramp-Up.** The Contractor shall use commercially reasonable
efforts to fill the agreed positions within **thirty (30) calendar
days** of the effective date. Partial months during ramp-up are
prorated by calendar day.

**Ramp-Down.** Either party may reduce the team size by providing
**thirty (30) days** written notice per team member to be removed.
The retainer adjusts from the first day of the next calendar month
following the notice period.

**Replacement Guarantee.** If a team member becomes unavailable
(resignation, illness, or performance issue), the Contractor shall
provide a replacement of comparable skill within **fifteen (15)
business days**. During the vacancy period, the retainer for that
position is reduced by **50%**.

**Performance Reviews.** The Customer may request a performance
review for any team member at any time. If the Customer reasonably
determines that a team member does not meet the required skill
level, the Contractor shall replace that member within **fifteen
(15) business days** of written notice.

**Overtime.** Work beyond the standard hours defined in the Team
Plan requires prior written approval from the Customer and is
billed at **125%** of the standard hourly rate derived from the
monthly retainer.

## How this clause works in practice

The dedicated-team model is a middle ground between staff augmentation
(pure hourly billing) and project-based delivery (fixed scope). The
monthly retainer gives the client cost predictability while the
replacement guarantee gives them quality assurance.

The 50% vacancy reduction is deliberately generous to the client —
it incentivizes the contractor to fill vacancies fast rather than
collect full retainer during gaps.
