## Maya Lindqvist

**Maya** is a freelance marketer we brought on in mid-2025 through a standard [[Subcontract Marketing 2026|marketing subcontract]]. She works across European e-commerce and boutique consumer brands — a sector we don't usually touch — and her value to us is almost entirely her **network**, not her hours. She is the opposite of **[[cast/jordi-costa|Jordi Costa]]**: where Jordi is long-serving institutional memory for one account, Maya is a door-opener whose scope of work changes every quarter.

She is also the unintentional subject of **[[marketing-referral-scope-2026]]** — the case that forced us to notice that our subcontractor template didn't actually say anything useful about who owns leads that a freelancer brings in from their pre-existing network. We wrote the Lead Attribution pattern because of her.

## How we met her

Referral from another freelancer. She came in cheap for a three-month content sprint, did the job, and instead of quietly moving on she wrote us a one-page strategy memo for a client we'd been struggling with. The memo was right. We kept her on retainer.

## What she pushes on

- **Creative autonomy.** Don't micromanage her brand voice. If you do, she will be polite and then deliver something that is, technically, what you asked for and, actually, worse than what she would have done.
- **Rate clarity up front.** She does not chase invoices, but she will not start work without a rate, a cap, and a clear scope line. This is unusual for marketers and we appreciate it.

## What she doesn't push on

- **IP.** She signs our [[Client IP Assignment]] without a word. She's been burned by it before — she told us so, once — but she's made her peace with it.
- **Publicity.** Happy to be listed, happy to be quoted. Her own personal brand benefits from being visible, and that's a rare alignment of interest.

## The thing about her network

Maya knows roughly 40 founders in European consumer e-commerce and has worked with most of them at least once, sometimes as an employee, sometimes as a freelancer, sometimes as a favour. When she brings us a client, **it is almost never a first meeting for her**. This is the awkward shape of **[[marketing-referral-scope-2026]]** — the client was new to _us_ but not new to _her_, and the question of who owns that relationship doesn't have a natural legal answer.

We have not solved this yet. Our current approach is: new **Lead Attribution** language in [[Subcontract Marketing 2026]] (in backlog — not yet written), plus a standing referral-fee policy, plus a disclosure requirement at onboarding. It's a workable patch, not a clean solution.
