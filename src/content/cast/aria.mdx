## Aria Media Holdings Ltd

**Aria** is the warmest voice on our client list and the hardest invoice to collect. Their account manager sends Christmas cards. Their finance team sends silence. The gap between those two behaviours is where every hour of **[[cast/jordi-costa|Jordi Costa]]**'s PM time went in 2024.

We love working with Aria creatively. Their editorial team is sharp, their briefs are thorough, and their feedback loops are tight. We just cannot cash their invoices without a reminder, a second reminder, a Legal letter, and — once — a formal adjudication.

## Why they hire us

Branded-content production pipelines, podcast infrastructure, and a custom CMS for their editorial teams. The work is stable, the scope changes often, and the margins are fine when paid on time.

## What they push on

- **Payment dates.** Every invoice. Every time. Their finance team runs on a rolling "next week" that has never, in our history together, arrived on the day they said it would. This is not a negotiating tactic — it's an operations problem they don't have the will to fix.
- **Scope creep without CR.** Their editorial team will add a feature over Slack and forget it's a contractual thing. Our PM is trained to convert every Slack ask into a change request with a price tag, which they hate but pay.
- **Rate increases.** They contest every rate rise, even the ones inside our **10% cap**. They don't win, but they cost us three calls per review cycle.

## What they don't push on

- **IP.** They don't care about the code — they care about the podcast episodes and the audience data. IP assignment goes through in one read.
- **Publicity.** They want to be named. They enjoy being quoted as a client in case studies. This is unusual for us and we use it carefully.

## Story threads

- 2024 — **[[late-payment-dispute-2024]]**. The adjudication case. Every clause in our payments stack got tested end-to-end, and every clause held. This is the case we point to when anyone asks "does our late-payment language actually do anything?". Yes. It does ~£70k worth.
- 2025 — **[[rate-increase-conversation-2025]]**. **[[cast/jordi-costa|Jordi]]** was their PM by then. We had to raise his rate under our 10% cap while simultaneously raising Aria's rate — which Aria fought — because otherwise Jordi was leaving for market.
- Ongoing — **[[cast/jordi-costa|Jordi]]** remains on the Aria account as of 2026. He understands their finance rhythm better than their finance team does, which is a real productivity moat and a succession risk all at once.

## Who they send

Their account-side contact is the Head of Editorial, who is lovely and powerless on finance questions. Their finance contact is a shared inbox that responds in ~9 business days. We always CC both — that combo gets faster answers than either in isolation.
