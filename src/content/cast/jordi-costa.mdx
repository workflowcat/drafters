## Jordi Costa

**Jordi** is the longest-running external PM we have. He joined us in late 2023 as a senior PM on the **[[cast/aria|Aria Media Holdings Ltd]]** account, signed a standard [[Subcontract PM 2026|PM subcontract]], and as of 2026 is still there. He has outlasted two of Aria's own project managers and one of our account managers. The Aria editorial team addresses him as if he works for them, which is its own kind of success and its own kind of risk.

He is the subject of **[[rate-increase-conversation-2025]]** — the case study we reach for whenever someone asks how our 10% rate-review cap actually plays out in practice. Short version: it played out exactly as designed, but only because we had the client-side 12% cap on the other side.

## What he cares about

- **Not being surprised.** If you move a deadline without asking him first, he will not complain — he will quietly rewrite the whole plan and send it to you on a Monday. This is worse than complaining. We learned.
- **Weekly recaps.** He writes one every Friday. We archive them. They are, collectively, the only reliable record of what Aria actually agreed to in any given month.
- **Fair rate reviews.** He asked once, in 2025, for a raise above our standard cap. We couldn't go above the cap — that's what the cap is for — but we found a way to get him most of the way there over two review cycles. See the case.

## What he doesn't care about

- **Travel.** He works from Barcelona, visits London twice a year, and never once has asked for an office-day budget.
- **Titles.** His Slack handle has been "jordi" since 2023. He has declined two title upgrades.

## Story threads

- 2025 — **[[rate-increase-conversation-2025]]**. The rate review we had to design our way through. The side-letter mechanism we invented for Jordi is now part of our **[[Rate Increase]]** pattern library — it's how we absorb shocks between our subcontractor-side cap and our client-side cap without renegotiating anything.
- Ongoing — Jordi is our unofficial institutional memory for Aria's finance quirks. He is, functionally, the thing preventing **[[late-payment-dispute-2024]]**-style escalations from happening a second time. This is a skill we have not yet figured out how to transfer to anyone else.

## Who Jordi is _not_

He is not our employee. He is a long-running independent subcontractor. The distance between "long-running" and "employee" is, for a Spanish subcontractor working for an English-law entity on behalf of a UK client, a non-trivial legal distance that Legal checks on quarterly. So far, the pattern holds: defined scope, own tools, own hours, multiple clients. We are careful to keep it that way.

## Signature quirks

- Every weekly recap starts with "Quick one:". They are never quick.
- Has a standing rule: no meetings between 13:00 and 15:00 CET. Lunch is lunch.
- Once sent an Aria project manager a Gantt chart as a _response to a panicked Slack message_. It worked.
