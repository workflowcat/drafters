## Dr. Lena Krivonos

**Lena** is the reason we treat contracts like runnable specifications. The first time we sent her an MSA, she sent it back with a diff. The diff was clean, minimal, and caught an inconsistency between our liability clause and our termination clause that we hadn't noticed in three years of use. She is the only client-side reader who has ever made us change our template for the better.

She is not a lawyer. She was trained as a distributed-systems researcher and ran academic infrastructure before her first CTO role. Her mental model of a contract is "a state machine that has to terminate cleanly in every path". If you send her an MSA with a path that doesn't terminate cleanly, she will find it and she will say so without heat.

## Where she shows up

- **[[cast/horizon|Horizon Maritime Analytics Oy]]** (2023–early 2025) — interim CTO through Phase 1 of their platform rebuild. This is the engagement that produced **[[milestone-ip-transfer-2025]]** — the milestone-vs-rolling IP debate. Her position on that debate was "I don't care which mechanism you use, I care that the termination path is specified". That reframing is what unstuck the negotiation.
- **[[cast/kestrel|Kestrel Health Systems, Inc.]]** (early 2025 → present) — CTO. She took the job after Horizon's Phase 1 shipped and she wanted a bigger problem. Kestrel got her, and we got a warm introduction into a client we probably couldn't have landed cold.

## What she pushes on

- **Termination paths.** Every clause, every time. "What happens if either side walks away right now?" is her default question. We now ask ourselves that question before she does.
- **Handover specificity.** She was the catalyst for our SLA handover addendum, which is now baked in as standard for any new client with IP/continuity anxiety. See [[milestone-ip-transfer-2025]].
- **Data residency as a design question.** Not just legal residency — she cares where backups live, where CI runners run, where observability data is exported. Most CTOs don't ask this. She does.

## What she doesn't push on

- **Price.** If the work is valuable, she pays without friction.
- **Marketing.** She will not be quoted. She will not appear in a case study. She will not speak at a conference _about_ us. She will, privately, recommend us to her network, which is how **[[cast/kestrel|Kestrel]]** happened.

## Story threads

- The Horizon-to-Kestrel move is a plot thread in its own right. We followed her, not the company. This is a recurring pattern in our business — **people carry relationships, companies don't** — and Lena is the clearest example.
- 2026 — **[[horizon-phase-2-precedent-2026]]**. Exactly what we warned about: Horizon's new Head of Engineering invoked Lena-era precedents during Phase 2 prep. We had to explain that some of those precedents were _her_ asks, not Horizon's, and rewrote the handover addendum under the new context.
- 2026 — **[[client-to-client-poach-non-solicit-2026]]**. Horizon's new HoE asked us, off the record, whether our non-solicit could "get Lena back" from Kestrel. The answer was no, for several reasons — the most important being that Lena moved before Kestrel was even our client. We used the episode to tighten our own rules about cross-client references.
