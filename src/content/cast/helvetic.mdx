## Helvetic Mutual Re AG

**Helvetic** is the client we point to when new people ask "what does a proper enterprise engagement look like?". They are not loud. They do not move quickly. They sign what they sign and then they pay on day 45 — every time. The legal department answers in nine business days. The architecture review board meets on Thursdays. Every clause in our contract with them has been through the committee at least twice.

## Why they hire us

Long-lived backend engagements inside their claims-handling and reinsurance-ledger systems. PCI-adjacent integrations. Small but high-stakes change windows. Any failure here costs real money for real policyholders, so the contract is not theatre — every clause has a job.

## What they push on

- **Liability cap.** They push hard to fix the cap at a specific number (€100–150k), not a formula. Our default of "12 months fees" spooks their committee because the number changes year to year. We have learned to meet them at **€150k fixed**, carving out wilful misconduct, IP infringement, and confidentiality breach from that cap — which is the pattern now baked into `[[liability-limitation-mutual]]`.
- **Late-payment interest.** They will ask for 0.25% daily (half our default) and frame it as a procurement policy. We usually give it to them because they actually pay. This is one case where we let go of the hard version.
- **Publicity.** No case studies, no logos, no "trusted by". They pay more instead.

## Story threads

- 2025 — **[[liability-cap-negotiation-2025]]**. First time we ran the €150k-plus-carveouts pattern with them. The precedent is now a baseline for any liability negotiation we do.
- Their CISO insists on a 2-business-day "delete all data" SLA on termination. We comply; it shows up as an addendum in every Helvetic MSA.
- **Phase 2 (backlog)** — a repeat engagement planned for 2026 will test whether "we already agreed to this" counts as leverage for other clients trying to copy the €150k cap.

## Who they send

Two people do all our communications with Helvetic: a head of IT procurement (we only know his email signature) and a senior architect who actually understands what we're building. Neither of them ever writes anything in a hurry.
