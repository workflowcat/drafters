## Atelier Brun SAS

**Atelier Brun** is the client who reminds us that small clients are not "enterprise but cheaper" — they are a different animal. There is no procurement. There is no legal team. There is one founder, her business partner, and a rotating cast of freelancers who come and go via **[[cast/maya-lindqvist|Maya Lindqvist]]**'s Rolodex. They take two weeks to answer the kind of email a Nordwind procurement officer would bounce in nine hours.

We keep them because the work is beautiful, the margins are fair, and they never negotiate — not because they agree, but because they don't know you're supposed to.

## Why they hire us

Their e-commerce stack: a bespoke Shopify-plus build, an inventory integration with a small Italian warehouse, and a recommendation engine that actually gets tested against a human editor's opinion. Everything they ship is hand-picked. Nothing they ship is automated. Their brand depends on that distinction.

## What they push on

- **Visual fidelity.** Élise will hold a release for 10 days over a 2-pixel spacing issue. This is not a bug; it is the brand. We have learned to budget design review into sprints.
- **Respect for the small team.** They want a single named contact on our side who knows them, remembers the last conversation, and doesn't rotate. We accommodate — it's usually our PM on a small carve-out.

## What they don't push on

- **Contract language.** They will sign almost anything that isn't obviously predatory. We use our **standard** [[Services Agreement Sloboda 2026|MSA]] with them without edits, and their version of "negotiation" is "Élise reads it on a Sunday and sends back one question".
- **Price.** They don't negotiate rates down. They just negotiate scope down — which is healthier, because it forces us to focus.
- **Payment.** SEPA transfer, 30 days, always on time. No drama.

## Story threads

- 2026 — **[[marketing-referral-scope-2026]]**. This is the case that made us write the **Lead Attribution** clause into our subcontract. **[[cast/maya-lindqvist|Maya]]** had been freelancing for Atelier Brun's previous e-commerce vendor back in 2022 (we didn't know), reconnected with Élise independently, and brought Atelier Brun to us as a new client. Who owns that lead? Whose name is on the referral fee? The case is about what [[Subcontract Marketing 2026]] did and didn't cover.
- We use Atelier Brun as the "small client" foil in our internal negotiation training — the case where the _absence_ of procurement is itself a risk, because there's no one on the other side to push back on clauses that are bad for _them_. We've had to talk Élise out of agreeing to things we didn't want her to agree to. This is a weird position to be in.

## Who they send

**Élise Brun** (founder, designer, reads everything, signs everything, answers at 11pm) and **[[cast/maya-lindqvist|Maya Lindqvist]]** (our freelance marketer, who is effectively their external marketing lead too — which is the whole awkward shape of the referral case). There is no one else.
