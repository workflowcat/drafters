**Reimbursable Expenses.** The Customer shall reimburse the
Contractor for reasonable, pre-approved expenses incurred in
connection with the Services. Expenses exceeding **€500** per
item require the Customer's prior written approval.

**Categories.** Typical reimbursable expenses include:

- Travel and accommodation (pre-approved business trips only)
- Software licenses and cloud infrastructure costs incurred
  specifically for the Customer's project
- Hardware purchased at the Customer's request
- Third-party services engaged with the Customer's approval

**Documentation.** The Contractor shall submit expense reports
monthly, accompanied by original receipts or equivalent
documentation. Expense reports are due within **ten (10) business
days** of the end of each calendar month.

**Payment.** Approved expenses are included in the next regular
invoice and are subject to the same payment terms as service fees.

**Non-Reimbursable.** The following are not reimbursable unless
explicitly agreed in writing:

- General overhead (office space, utilities, internet)
- Internal training and professional development
- Equipment used across multiple clients
- Penalties, fines, or charges resulting from the Contractor's
  negligence

## How this clause works in practice

Expense reimbursement is clean in theory but messy in execution.
The €500 pre-approval threshold is calibrated to catch significant
costs (flights, licenses) without creating friction on small ones
(a €30 domain registration).

Monthly reconciliation prevents the "surprise invoice six months
later" problem — expenses are current and auditable.

- Not used in dedicated team (expenses are part of the retainer)
